Federal tax — §280E framework

  • 26 U.S.C. § 280E — statute denying business expense deductions for trafficking in controlled substances (cannabis remains Schedule I federally)
  • 26 U.S.C. § 471 — inventory accounting rules; basis for COGS treatment that remains deductible despite §280E
  • C-corp federal rate: 21% flat — Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 (TCJA), permanent for corporate income tax
  • Pass-through structures (LLC, S-corp): owner pays federal tax at individual rates 24–37% on disallowed amounts; substantially worse §280E exposure than C-corp

Methodology notes

  • 5-year MCS cost lock: Per user-supplied assumption that Blue Ox costs remain flat over 5 years, the entire MCS bucket (annual fee + lease + admin + trim/processing) is held at year-1 dollar values across all 5 years. The MCS escalator slider lets you stress-test contract escalation if applicable.
  • Year-1 setup haircut: Brand & marketing (60%), professional fees (35%), and sales operation (20%) are reduced for years 2–5 to reflect one-time setup costs (logo design, package design, entity formation, application support, account prospecting).
  • Volume scaling: Packaging units, transportation, vape hardware (B-retail), and bad debt allowance scale with yield/revenue year-over-year. Lab testing, lease, admin, insurance, banking, and most professional fees stay flat.
  • Tax treatment: Federal §280E applied to gross profit (revenue − COGS). MN state tax applied to real net income (revenue − COGS − opex) per MN cannabis income tax subtraction. 15% MN cannabis gross receipts tax is retail-only and does NOT apply to wholesale operations modeled here.
